Butter Chicken

Description

Butter Chicken also known as Chicken Makhani is a classic Indian dish that's made by simmering marinated
and grilled chicken in a spicy, aromatic, buttery and creamy tomato gravy. This lip smacking and delicious
dish is hugely popular among the Indian food lovers across the world.

It is believed that the Butter Chicken was invented during 1930 to 1935 at a resturant in India. The resturant
would usually have large orders of tandoori chicken but sometimes ended up with leftovers which were dry.

Ingredients:-

  • 500 gm of boneless chicken
  • 3 tbsp yogurt
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • Salt as per taste
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 inch ginger (grated)
  • 2 green chilies (finely chopped)
  • 1 tbsp oil

Steps:-

  1. In a large bowl, mix together the yogurt, lemon juice, turmeric powder, red chili powder, cumin powder,
    coriander powder, salt, minced garlic, grated ginger, and finely chopped green chilies.
  2. Add the chicken pieces to the marinade mixture and coat them evenly. Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at
    least 2 hours or overnight for better flavor absorption.
  3. Preheat your oven to 200°C (400°F). If you have a broiler function on your oven, use it instead of baking in this step.
  4. Remove the chicken from the marinade and place it on a lined baking tray. Drizzle some oil over the chicken pieces.
  5. Bake the chicken in the preheated oven for about 20 minutes, or until they are cooked through and slightly charred.
    You can also broil the chicken for an additional 5 minutes if desired.
  6. Serve the tandoori chicken hot with fresh coriander leaves and lemon wedges for garnishing. Enjoy!

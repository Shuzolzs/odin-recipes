Shukto

Description

Shukto is a popular vegetable dish in Indian cuisine usually served with rice. The tradition
of starting a meal by consuming bitters, considered to have a medicinal value, dates back to ancient
times. Shukto was consumed as a cooling agent in the hot and humid climates of the ancient kingdoms
of undivided Bengal like Anga, Vanga and Kalinga.

Ingredients:-

  • A mix of seasonal vegetables (e.g., bottle gourd, pumpkin, brinjal, potato)
  • Mustard oil
  • Turmeric powder
  • Cumin seeds
  • Fenugreek seeds
  • Poppy seeds
  • Green chillies
  • Ginger paste
  • Garlic paste
  • Salt
  • Sugar
  • Coriander leaves for garishing

Steps:-

  1. Wash and chop the selected vegetables into bite-sized pieces. Set aside.
  2. Heat mustard oil in a large pan or wok. Once hot, add turmeric powder, cumin seeds,
    fenugreek seeds, green chillies, ginger paste, and garlic paste. Fry until fragrant.
  3. Add the chopped vegetables to the pan and stir well to coat them with the spice mixture.
    Cover the pan and cook on medium heat until the vegetables soften.
  4. Grind poppy seeds into a smooth paste using water. Add this paste to
    the pan and mix thoroughly. Cook for a few more minutes.
  5. Season the dish with salt and sugar according to taste. Stir well to combine all ingredients.
  6. Remove the cover and simmer the shukto until the vegetables are tender and the gravy thickens slightly.
  7. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves just before serving.
